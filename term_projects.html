Linear theory of Faraday instability in viscous fluids

Advisor: Prof. Krishna Kumar, Department of Physics, IIT Kharagpur | Feb 2020 - June 2020

For the course Fluid Mechanics in my 4th semester, in this project I studied the onset of Faraday Instability, i.e eventual ramp up of surface wave patterns in a fluid being oscillated vertically. For this I first linearized the Navier-Stokes variant for the surface displacement field then took the spatial Fourier transform. In the Fourier space now I used Floquet analysis to get the dynamics of the continuum of modes. Finally by reduction of the dynamics to a finite discrete set of Fourier modes, thereby represting it by a matrix, I computed the instability diagrams via the eigenvalues.

Turbulence in Exciton Polariton Condensates

Charpak Lab Scholar at PhLAM, CNRS| May 2022 - July 2022

The aim of the project was to perform a time resolved study of superfluid of exciton-polariton condensate flowing against a defect acting as an obstacle to study vortex shedding and eventually tubulence. Under the guidance of CNRS Researcher Alberto Amo this was my first experimental project and I got the oppurtunity to build up everything from the very scratch, starting from optical allignments, electronics, calibrating the optomechanics and cryogenics, characterizing the dispersion curves and condensation thresholds using Fourier space spectroscopy to finally probing the desired phenomena. The internship was funded by the Charpak Lab Scholarship awarded to 30 students by the French Embassy in India.

Theoretical and Experimental study of nonequilibrium physics in steady state exciton polariton condensates

Advisor: Prof. Vishwanath Shukla and Prof. Sajal Dhara, Department of Physics, IIT Kharagpur | Aug 2022 - March 2023

Having developed a robust code to simulate coherent and incoherent Bose condensation of exciton-polaritons in a microcavity, we have headed on to theoretically model nonequilbrium physics like KPZ or BKT phenomena in 2D polariton condensates in microcavities having additional features like anisotropy or disorder. We are parallelly designing the experiments to characterize these in steady states i.e without using time resolution. The theoretical quantum fluidic parts of the project are being advised by Prof. Vishwanath Shukla while the experimental realization shall be done under the supervision of Prof. Sajal Dhara

In the right, you see our simulation of an incoherent condensation of polaritons using a Gaussian pump in presence of disorder, all tuned to produce Airy like waves slowly stabilizing to steady circular fringes and a nearly constant total number of polaritons. Also we see interference of these Airy waves with the reflected waves from boundaries in the later stages of simulation which eventually produces turbulent behaviour.
