Linear theory of Faraday instability in viscous fluids

Advisor: Prof. Krishna Kumar, Department of Physics, IIT Kharagpur | Feb 2020 - June 2020

For the course Fluid Mechanics in my 4th semester, in this project I studied the onset of Faraday Instability, i.e eventual ramp up of surface wave patterns in a fluid being oscillated vertically. For this I first linearized the Navier-Stokes variant for the surface displacement field then took the spatial Fourier transform. In the Fourier space now I used Floquet analysis to get the dynamics of the continuum of modes. Finally by reduction of the dynamics to a finite discrete set of Fourier modes, thereby represting it by a matrix, I computed the instability diagrams via the eigenvalues.
