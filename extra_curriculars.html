Ongoing Master's Thesis Project

Theoretical and Experimental study of nonequilibrium physics in steady state exciton polariton condensates

Advisor: Prof. Vishwanath Shukla and Prof. Sajal Dhara, Department of Physics, IIT Kharagpur | Aug 2022 - March 2023

Having developed a robust code to simulate coherent and incoherent Bose condensation of exciton-polaritons in a microcavity, we have headed on to theoretically model nonequilbrium physics like KPZ and BKT phenomena in 2D polariton condensates having additional features like anisotropy. We are parallelly designing the experiments to characterize these in steady states i.e without using time resolution. The theoretical quantum fluidic parts of the project are being advised by Prof. Vishwanath Shukla while the experimental realization shall be done under the supervision of Prof. Sajal Dhara

In the right, you see our simulation of an incoherent condensation of polaritons using a Gaussian pump in presence of disorder, all tuned to produce Airy like waves slowly stabilizing to steady circular fringes and a nearly constant total number of polaritons. Also we see interference of these Airy waves with the reflected waves from boundaries in the later stages of simulation which eventually produces turbulent behaviour.
